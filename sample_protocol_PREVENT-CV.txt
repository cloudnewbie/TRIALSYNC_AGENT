CLINICAL STUDY PROTOCOL: PREVENT-CV
Protocol Number: P-2026-CV-001
Sponsor: Demo Pharma Inc.
IND Number: 184,392
Therapeutic Area: Cardiovascular Disease / Cardiology
Study Title: A Phase 3, Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled Study to Evaluate the Efficacy and Safety of CV-9081 in Reducing Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events (MACE) in Adults with Coronary Atherosclerosis and Hypercholesterolemia (PREVENT-CV)

---

1. OBJECTIVES AND CLINICAL RATIONALE
Major Adverse Cardiovascular Events (MACE) remain the leading cause of death globally. Hypercholesterolemia, particularly elevated Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL) cholesterol, accelerates the progression of coronary artery atherosclerosis. 
This study (PREVENT-CV) evaluates the novel PCSK9-inhibitor CV-9081 administered subcutaneously in combination with high-intensity statin therapy. The goal is to determine if the addition of CV-9081 reduces the incidence of cardiovascular death, myocardial infarction, and stroke compared to statin therapy alone.

2. TARGET ENROLLMENT & GEOGRAPHY
The study aims to enroll a total of 500 patients globally across designated clinical trial sites. 
Participating regions include:
- United States (US)
- Canada (CA)

3. PATIENT SELECTION: ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

3.1 INCLUSION CRITERIA
Subjects must meet all of the following criteria at the screening and baseline visits to be eligible for study enrollment:
1. AGE: Subject must be an adult aged 45 years and older at the time of signing the informed consent (specifically between 45 and 85 years of age).
2. INDICATION / DIAGNOSIS: Subject must have a documented diagnosis of coronary atherosclerosis of native coronary artery. Diagnostic evidence must be supported by an authentic ICD-10 code:
   - ICD-10 Code: I25.10 (Coronary atherosclerosis of native coronary artery)
3. LAB VALUES (LIPIDS): Subject must present with severe hypercholesterolemia. Fasting lipid panels conducted at the screening visit must indicate:
   - Low-Density Lipoprotein (LDL) Cholesterol > 190 mg/dL (5.0 mmol/L).
4. BACKGROUND THERAPY: Subject must be on a stable regimen of high-intensity statin therapy (atorvastatin 40-80 mg daily or rosuvastatin 20-40 mg daily) for at least 6 weeks prior to screening.

3.2 EXCLUSION CRITERIA
Subjects meeting any of the following criteria at screening or baseline will be excluded from the study:
1. ACUTE MYOCARDIAL INFARCTION: Subject has experienced an acute myocardial infarction (MI), unstable angina, coronary artery bypass graft (CABG), or percutaneous coronary intervention (PCI) within 6 months prior to the screening visit (< 6 months lookback period).
2. RENAL FAILURE: Subject has a history of severe renal impairment or end-stage renal disease indicating glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) < 15 mL/min/1.73m2 or requiring active renal dialysis.
3. CONCOMITANT MEDICATIONS: Active use of other experimental lipid-lowering compounds or investigational therapeutic agents within 30 days prior to the baseline visit.
4. UNCONTROLLED HYPERTENSION: Resting systolic blood pressure > 160 mmHg or diastolic blood pressure > 100 mmHg.

4. STUDY ASSESSMENTS AND PROCEDURE SCHEDULE
All clinical sites must be equipped with specialized procedural equipment and qualified personnel. Required procedural assessments include:
1. ELECTROCARDIOGRAM (ECG): A standard 12-lead resting electrocardiogram (ECG) must be conducted at the screening visit, the baseline visit, and at all subsequent follow-up visits (Months 1, 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, and 18).
2. ECHOCARDIOGRAM: A complete transthoracic echocardiogram (Echocardiogram) is required to evaluate left ventricular function and ejection fraction. Echocardiograms must be performed on-site by certified technicians at:
   - Baseline (Day 0)
   - Month 6
   - Month 12
   - Study Completion (Month 18)
3. LIPID PANELS: Fasting lipid panel assessments (measuring LDL-C, HDL-C, Total Cholesterol, and Triglycerides) must be processed by the local or central laboratory at:
   - Screening
   - Baseline
   - Every 3 months (Months 3, 6, 9, 12, 15, and 18)
